My Journey Through UW

Benjamin Brusniak Honors Portfolio

Reflecting on all the experiences I have gained throughout college, I would say that the central theme for me has been exploration. Although I came to the University of Washington with a major and the Honors Program already in mind, I do not think that I could have possibly anticipated the breadth of experiences that I was able to have. Whether it was studying abroad, being a teaching assistant, taking a class on Russian Murder Mysteries, or so many other adventures I was able to undertake, I think that this diversity of experiences will be what I remember most fondly, and miss the most, after I leave.

And so, to manifest this theme of exploration into my Honors Portfolio, I have decided to create distinct sections for the viewer to experience. I have decided to break my portfolio into classes, clubs, work experiences, and general life experiences. This way, the viewer can navigate the portfolio depending on which side of my exploration they want to see. Interested in my academic adventures? Check out some of the Computer Science courses I have taken. Intrigued by my professional or teaching life? Simply navigate my reflections on my internships and teaching assistant jobs.

However, although I have loved many of my experiences in college, I think that college can also be a time to prune down what I want to chase after and experience in life. As a reflection tool, I think that it is important that my exploration does not go to waste. And so, I would like my portfolio to not just display the things I have experienced and achieved, but also how I can use that experience to inform how I would like to organize and live my life going forward. In this way, the portfolio can serve as a tangible benchmark from which I can prioritize my goals in the future, leveraging the wide base of experiences that college has provided for me.

To state this pruning most succinctly, I think that I should prioritize the following things as I emerge into my adult life:

  • Software Industry Work
  • Engaging with the community around me, preferably through teaching
  • Continuing to build and nurture personal relationships
  • Continuing to work on my personal health and wellness, both mentally and physically

I hope that anyone looking through my portfolio in the future can understand why I have chosen these things to prioritize, and that they can see the wonderful breadth of experience that the University of Washington and my family has been able to provide for me.
